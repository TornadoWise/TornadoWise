Project Scope

The scope of this project is to design and implement an interactive web-based map that consolidates and visualizes critical geospatial data related to tornadoes for communities in Southern Canada. The map will leverage open datasets, including tornado occurrence points and tracks, land cover, building footprints, average home values, climate anomalies, weather station data, and topographic features, to deliver actionable insights to both residents and policymakers. Key deliverables include:
  • An online interactive map with selectable data layers tailored to different user groups (residents vs. policymakers)
  • Concise user guidance and resources for navigating and interpreting the map

Our goal

The primary goal of this project is to increase tornado risk awareness, preparedness, and decision-making capability among Canadian residents and policymakers. By transforming complex geospatial datasets into clear, accessible, and interactive visualizations, the project aims to:
  • Empower residents to understand their personal and community-level tornado risks, identify nearby hazards, and locate critical infrastructure
  • Support policymakers in identifying areas at risk, prioritizing resource allocation, and developing targeted emergency management policies
  • Foster public engagement and trust through transparency and data-driven communication
